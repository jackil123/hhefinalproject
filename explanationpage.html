Thank you for trying out the environmental health simulator! Here is a page explaining in detail how the choices you made affected your life, and lead to either your good health or ill health.

For the first choice, you had the option to choose produce. The main environmental health concern in this scenario is the presence of pesticides in the produce we buy. Pesticides make their way from the fields into our kitchens, and have been shown to cause serious health problems. According to the WHO, "[Pesticides] may induce adverse health effects including cancer, effects on reproduction, immune or nervous systems." Certain crops require more pesticides and harmful chemicals in the growing process, meaning they are less safe to eat. For the second choice, you had the option to choose seafood. All seafood has mercury in it but due to a process known as biomagnification, the higher up the food chain and the larger the marine animal is the higher the concentration of mercury it contains. Because of this, it is safer to eat smaller fish, and due to the presence of POPs (persisten organic pollutants), fish from certain parts of the world are safer. For the third choice, you had the option to choose from several different locations around the world to reside in. All the possible options have unsafe levels of air pollution, but Riyadh, Saudi Arabia has slightly better air quality than Peshawar and Onitsha. Peshawar and Onitsha have two of the worst air quality ratings in the world. If you would like to know more about what cities are more or less polluted, click HERE

For the fourth choice, you had the option to choose form several different methods of hydration. In a country that doesn't often have the cleanest water, it is important to choose the right source from which you will drink. Bottled water can cause sickness, because the plastics and other materials it is stored in can leach into the water stored inside, in the form of PCBs and other endocrine-disrupting chemicals. Tap water is safer, but it is often treated, and sourced from areas that might be polluted. The safest bet is water from a well you dug far away from any development. The natural hydrological processes that bring water to aquifers and then back up to the surface have a filtering effect, creating clean, drinkable and safe water. For the fifth choice, you now had to choose how you would earn a living in your new home. Many jobs, espcially those that include hard labor (those which are most available to immigrants), carry with them occupational hazards, many of which come from the chemicals that you would come into contact with on a day to day basis. As we learned in the first choice, modern day farming requires heavy use of strong pesticides and herbicides, so as a farm laborer you would be exposed to these such chemicals, which can wreak havoc on your body.
